# Reward Contract

## Allowed rewards
- Short animation
- Gentle sound
- Spoken encouragement
- Parent-visible milestone
- "You figured it out" feedback
- Curiosity prompt after success

## Ownership Is Not A Reward

Customization belongs to the activity and the thing the child is making. It is
not a prize paid out after correct answers. Colors, stickers, placement, and
other expressive choices must be available without points, streaks, currency,
random unlocks, or repeated labor.

Random scenery variation is allowed when it does not alter access or learning
truth. Randomized access to customization is forbidden.

The completed result must satisfy
`docs/contracts/ownership-completion.contract.md`; praise, animation, confetti,
or a disconnected sticker chest cannot substitute for a child-owned result.

## Forbidden rewards
- Streaks
- Daily pressure
- Random loot boxes
- Infinite levels
- Punitive loss states
- Social comparison
- Leaderboards
- Ads
- Engagement bait
- Randomized customization access
- Scored or ranked expressive choices
- Currency-gated colors, stickers, or decorations
- Disconnected completion prizes presented as ownership

## Reward language
Prefer:
- "You found it."
- "You solved it."
- "You tried again."
- "That was a tricky one."
- "You noticed the pattern."

Avoid:
- "You are so smart."
- "You failed."
- "Wrong."
- "Try harder."
- "You lost."
