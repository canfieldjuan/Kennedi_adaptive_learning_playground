# v0.3.59 Ownership Completion Principle

This slice makes transformative agency and ownership completion permanent
product law, records the current game-by-game baseline honestly, and creates
the gate later retrofit slices must satisfy. It does not pretend the existing
games already comply.

## Before Code

### Root Cause

The product contracts reward completion and prohibit compulsive reward loops,
but they do not require a child action to change the play world, do not require
completion to leave behind a child-owned result, and do not require the payoff
to preserve the child's exact choices. That omission allowed otherwise safe,
playable games to finish with generic reactions, partial reconstructions, or
results that disappear when the route changes. The app can therefore record a
correct answer without giving the child durable authorship over what happened.

The reward contract also permits "unlocking a creative sticker after session
completion." That treats access to expression as payment, which conflicts with
the intended ownership model: customization belongs to the thing the child is
making and must not depend on points, streaks, currency, randomness, or repeated
labor.

### Correct Fix Must Touch

- `docs/contracts/ownership-completion.contract.md` (new) — define:
  - Child Principle #1: the child changes the world rather than merely
    answering questions about it;
  - Ownership Completion: the child finishes with something made, changed,
    personalized, or placed into the world;
  - exact-choice continuity through payoff and preservation;
  - the implementation questions and rejection tests;
  - one core learning action, one or two ownership actions, one payoff as the
    bounded default;
  - separation of expressive state from mastery/scoring;
  - local-first preservation, child-controlled inspection, and revisit without
    earning access again;
  - adoption rules for new games and the existing-game retrofit arc.
- `docs/contracts/product.contract.md` — promote transformative agency and
  ownership completion into the numbered product principles.
- `docs/contracts/reward.contract.md` — remove sticker unlocking as an allowed
  reward; distinguish ownership/personalization from payment; allow random
  scenery but forbid randomized access to expression.
- `docs/contracts/game-maturity.contract.md` — require mature primary games to
  create, preserve, and accurately replay a completion object while keeping
  the ownership layer bounded.
- `docs/contracts/game-intake.contract.md` — require incoming games to state how
  they satisfy or are scheduled to satisfy the ownership contract.
- `scripts/check-work-contract.mjs` — make the new permanent contract a
  repository gate by pinning its required normative sections and statements.
- `tests/contract/ownership-completion.test.ts` (new) — prove the product,
  reward, maturity, and intake contracts all point to the same rule; reject the
  old sticker-unlock language and payment mechanics as ownership substitutes.
- `MVP_BASELINE.md` — record that v0.3.59 adopts the principle while clearly
  marking the existing-game retrofit as open work rather than claiming
  compliance.
- `package.json` and `package-lock.json` — advance the local product baseline to
  `0.3.59`.
- This work contract — preserve the code-grounded baseline and ordered retrofit
  plan below.

### Must Not Change

  child-facing behavior changes in this principle slice.
- No event shape, event timing, mastery rule, transfer-strength rule,
  curriculum, recommendation, parent approval, or evidence interpretation
  changes.
- No storage key, migration, export payload, clear-data behavior, or gallery /
  history implementation changes yet.
- No backend, auth, cloud sync, open web, real camera, AI tutor, generative
  child content, reward economy, points, streaks, leaderboards, randomized
  unlocks, or public sharing.
- No dependency changes and no unrelated formatting or cleanup.
- Nature Camera Safari is absent from current `main`; this slice does not
  re-import or redesign it.

### Assumptions / Blockers

- "Primary game" currently means the four child-home worlds (Words, Bear Cafe,
  Number Train, Bear Art Studio) plus Story Stage. Legacy direct-route
  tap-choice/coloring activities remain supported but are not declared mature
  primary worlds by this slice.
- Local preservation may be a capped pure view over existing durable records or
  a narrow non-evaluative local record. Each retrofit must choose the smallest
  honest model that preserves the exact visible result and still participates
  in existing export/clear boundaries.
- Expressive choices may be carried as non-scored metadata or a separate
  non-evaluative record, but they must never produce correctness, mastery,
  transfer, ranking, or recommendation weight.
- A completion object need not be retained forever. It must survive the payoff,
  remain available long enough to admire, and be revisitable through a bounded
  local collection without re-earning access.
- Blockers: none for the principle slice. Every runtime retrofit remains open
  after this slice by design.

## Existing-Game Baseline

This baseline is derived from current `main`, not from roadmap claims.

| Primary game | What already works | Standing ownership gap |
|---|---|---|
| Bear Cafe | The child builds a tray; delivery and handoff retain selected food; completion metadata retains food/color/decoration ids. | Most orders offer no unscored packaging choice; delivery drops selected color/decoration; completion shows a generic bear; no child cafe wall or revisitable order object. |
| Words / Word Builder | Letter tiles remain in slots and the pictured object reacts after a completed build. | Initial-sound/blending modes remain answer selection; completed words do not alter the world beyond a generic pop; no unscored authorship choice or revisitable word stage. |
| Number Train | Load rounds let the child construct a quantity; missing-number rounds visibly repair the track. | Count rounds remain answer selection; no expressive train choice; arrival preserves only the current round, not the exact journey; no revisitable route map. |
| Bear Art Studio | The child creates and personalizes art; the bear reacts; a capped event-derived gallery persists locally. | The gallery descriptor does not preserve exact brush paths or sticker positions, so replay can substitute an approximation for the child's actual piece. |
| Story Stage | Choices cause authored branches and exact choice ids persist in non-evaluative local history. | The saved story is parent-facing only; the child has no story shelf or exact-path replay surface and cannot revisit the completed story without reconstructing it. |

## Ordered Retrofit Arc

Each item is a separate contract, worktree, and small PR. No later slice begins
until the operator explicitly orders it after the current PR merges.

1. Bear Cafe ownership completion: one lightweight packaging choice, exact bag
   continuity through delivery/customer possession, and a capped local cafe
   order wall derived without scoring expression.
2. Words transformative completion: concrete built words visibly alter the
   workshop, one bounded expressive placement/detail choice, and a capped local
   word stage preserving the exact result.
3. Number Train ownership completion: preserve the final loaded train, add one
   unscored conductor/flag choice after the core math action, travel with the
   exact train, and add a capped route-map marker.
4. Bear Art Studio exact preservation: replace approximate gallery replay with
   a bounded safe snapshot representation of the actual marks, fills, and
   sticker placements while preserving the existing evidence boundary.
5. Story Stage child preservation: add a child-facing story shelf and replay
   from the stored exact selections/choice path without grading imagination.
